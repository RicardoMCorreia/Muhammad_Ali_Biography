Olympic Gold Medal - Rome 1960

After starting boxing at 12 years old, Ali achieved his first major accolade by winning the gold medal (Light heavyweight - 175lbs) at the 1960 Summer Olympics, in Rome. Shortly after, Ali made his professional debut vs Tunney Hunsacker, in which he won via a 6 round decision. He would go on to compete 9 more times in the span of a year, amassing a record of 9-0 by 29 November 1961.

Muhammad Ali vs Sonny Liston
25 February 1964

Muhammad Ali first major victory as a professional came against heavy-hitter Sonny Liston, a man who had been undefeated for 28 straight bouts, spanning over a period of 10 years. Ali entered the match as an 8-1 underdog, but quickly proved to be too much for the 36 year old Liston, and the match ended in a 6th round stoppage in favour of the “People’s Champ”. Ali was 22 years old and had just shocked the world and claimed the heavyweight title in the process. Ali and Liston would end up fighting again 1 year and 3 months later, with the result being a 1st round stoppage for Ali

Draft refusal and banning from boxing

On 22 April 1967, after 8 successful consecutive title defences, Ali was drafted for military service in the Vietname War, which he refused. The consequences of this decision were that Ali was banned from boxing for 3 years, and sentenced to 5 years in prison. When questioned about his decision, he stated: “I’ve got nothing to lose by standing for my beliefs”. After an appeal was made, he was able to stay out of prison, but would not compete in boxing between the age of 25 and 29 years old.
